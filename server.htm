On premise Web-Server

Until recently my website was hosted on site with this little stand alone web-server. It's based on a Pic18F series MCU and the RTL 8019AS MAC. For an 8 bit MCU this little thing served up the web pages surprisingly fast. 

Of course it's not really meant as a personal web sever since it's file system is on a 64K serial flash chip. This becomes pretty limiting as you might guess. Maybe I work on interfacing a SD flash card to it to expand the capacity.

The real use of this device lies in remote monitoring of facilities such as swimming pools, small  businesses,  factories, etc.  where it is important or nice to know when something is not working as it should.

So, for example as in the case of a bakery owner it's good to get an early e-mail notification on the mobile phone when the walk in freezer is loosing it's temper because the AC unit gave out,  before all the wedding cakes melt. 

Or in the case of a pool manager it's helpful if the PH, Chlorine and temperature values can be monitored and adjusted via the web while on vacation in Tahiti.
