Weather Stations

Custom PIC based station with Temp and Wind data updating every 15 seconds.

     

Click on the displays above to see charted station details.

Click here to go to see the  local interface of the device

The wind sensors are all solid state using a hall effect devices instead of reed relay switches and potentiometers as found in the commercially available Davis Instrument weather stations.

For the anemometer  a 14 pole magnet housing  and a  bipolar Hall effect switch is used, giving 6 pulses per revolution. This in conjunction with the ball bearings used allows  the capturing of wind speeds down to about 0.5Mph. The magnet / bearing assembly is actually a RC model  DC motor with it's iron armature removed to provide smooth action. 

The wind vane is  based on a magnetic rotary encoder which is made off 4 hall effect units, producing a pulse width modulated signal that's directly proportional to the direction of magnetic flux over the device. The chosen device has a resolution of 1.4 deg (8 bits) , more than enough for wind direction. The vane is mounted in a roller bearing and also reacts to wind less than 1 Mph. as long as the bearings stay clean of corrosion and debris.  Time will tell !

The 3rd device  on the sensor  tree is a CIGS solar panel that measures the solar radiation during the day. I find this information useful as it indirectly shows cloud cover along with sunrise and sunset. 

The Barometer sensor is mounted on the  controller PCB. A standard  pressure sensor from FreeScale, feeding an ADC input of the PIC, is used.

The Temperature sensor is an LM334 remote temperature probe which feeds directly into an analog input of the PIC as well.

Humidity is measured with a Honywell HIH4030 calibrated humidity sensor.

The controller board is an SBC65 or SBC68 from an Australian company Modtronix. It has a PIC 18F1680 and a RTL8019 MAC. It's using the free TCIP/IP stack from Microchip with extension to act as an HTTP client and server.

The advantage of using an embedded device instead of a weather station / PC combination is that this setup only uses about 3W to operate and is absolutely quiet, and of course it is much more rewarding when you can say you made it yourself.
