The picture above shows my experimental RV8 airplane in flight near Bodega Bay, CA. I built this over a period of 8 years, mostly in my garage.  Coincidentally the airworthiness certificate was signed  by the FAA on my birthday 2004. The airplane won "Grand Champion Kit" award at the 2006 Golden West EAA Fly-in and was pictured in Van's "RV-Ator" magazine.

I elected to outfit this airplane with an Franklin 220 HP motor and a constant speed propeller, not a very common engine. I prefer this engine because of it smooth running, it's a 6 cylinder horizontal opposed and since I had experience rebuilding engines of the same make before.

Of course going with an engine that was never used in this kind of airplane meant that I got to do all the engineering work firewall forward myself. This meant that I had to learn 3D CAD first to design the engine mount, so that the propeller came out in the right spot.

In the end it was well worth the extra effort. The extra 20 to 40 HP more  gives it a comfortable cruise speed of 195 Mph at 75% power. I usually operate it at 62% where it still scoots along at 175 Mph with a fuel consumption of about 7.5Gal in the fully leaned condition.

In Summer of 2006 we took the airplane to Oshkosh WI. The trip took 10 Hrs each way and we got a gas mileage of around 22 Mpg even though we had stiff headwinds most of the time. It was a joy to fly, except when we got beaten up in turbulence between Salt Lake City and Reno.

My other Airplane is a 1947 Stinson Voyager. It's the family plane and we take it camping whenever we can. We had it for 15 years now and it's a great performer, not necessarily the fastest airplane around at 120mph, but you are definitely traveling in style wherever you go.
